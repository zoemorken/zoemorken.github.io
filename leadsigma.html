LeadSigma Internship

Designed LeadSigma's sales-tech platform to support small business growth.

Remote, Winter 2020 (3 months, November 2020–January 2021)

About LeadSigma

Based in Lenexa, Kansas, LeadSigma is a sales-tech platform supporting small businesses by connecting online forms (websites, landing pages, and/or digital lead forms) to business phones in order to immediately generate conversations with new customers and increase conversion rates. The platform also manages actionable follow-up tasks to ensure that sales teams execute the right follow-up actions at the right times.

My Role

I was the Lead UI/UX Designer for LeadSigma’s UI team, driving the design of the web application. LeadSigma Call Slingshot connects online contact forms with business phones, and LeadSigma SalesStream creates follow-up tasks that help sales teams connect with leads. Since SalesStream needed a way to display the tasks a sales representative should complete in a given day, we had to create an app that allowed users different ways to interact with their tasks.

My responsibilities included supervising and delegating tasks to the design interns, making key decisions on how features should be displayed within the app, and transforming wireframes into high-fidelity, interactive prototypes for customer demonstrations. As this position was remote with a hard deadline, our team relied heavily on communication and organization tools such as Slack and Notion to deliver quality work in a timely manner.

Web App Objectives

For the first release of LeadSigma (February 1), our team was to complete the four main pages of the web app: Dashboard, SalesStream, Pipeline, and Activity.

Dashboard
  • Preview of upcoming and overdue tasks
  • Relevant statistics to help salespeople track their progress on lead follow-up
SalesStream
  • List of all tasks needed to be completed in a day
  • Predicted list of tasks for coming days / weeks
  • Overview for a specific task associated with a lead, with a breakdown of their contact information and interaction with the business over time
  • Ways to call, email, or text a lead through the app
  • Notification informing the salesperson whether an inbound call has been received
  • Add a new lead into the task sequence
  • Ability to add both general or task-related notes about a lead
Pipeline
  • Top-level view of how the business is performing on lead follow-up and conversion
  • Relevant statistics to help salespeople track their progress on lead follow-up
  • Number of leads in the Pipeline as well as what stage they’re in (warm, cool, pending)
  • Detailed view of lead contact information and interaction with the business over time
Activity
  • Record of all activity conducted within the LeadSigma app (e.g. adding / removing a lead, marking a lead as won, lost, or pending, editing general or task-related notes for a lead)
  • Way to filter activity according to salesperson or type of activity
  • Activities LeadSigma conducted as part of the automated task sequence set by the business

Process

After being assigned to focus on the web app, my team went to work by sketching out various ways we could display the main pages. I drew inspiration from previous work completed by the Purdue UX students in the Fall 2020 semester, designs from a contracted product designer previously employed by LeadSigma, and online resources such as Dribbble. After several rounds of sharing our sketches with CEO Scott Hansen and CTO John Moses, we made necessary iterations then transitioned to using Whimsical to create mockups.

Once we came to an agreement on the designs, I built out high-fidelity versions of each page along with what would happen when users would interact with certain elements (hovering, clicking on a button, etc.). This was completed in Figma. The developers then built the app front end based on the comps I’d created.

Reflection

  • Frequently ask for feedback on designs from others on the team: Because I worked closely with Scott and John, it was imperative that what I created aligned with their vision of the web app functionality. Though it’s important to test with external stakeholders, receiving input from team members was just as necessary.
  • Make sure the focus is on the person, not the design: Since my work was primarily on the UI of the app, it was easy to get lost in the details rather than view the project at a holistic level. To mitigate this, I would frequently revisit LeadSigma’s vision. Asking questions like “What is the salesperson trying to achieve when placed in [X] situation?” or “What functionalities would be important to highlight on this page?” were important in helping me zoom out and not get stuck on my design preferences.

I had a wonderful remote internship experience at LeadSigma. I am so grateful for the opportunity to work on an app that has the true ability to transform the way small businesses operate. Despite being remote, our team built strong relationships during our impromptu and scheduled Google Meets. A special thanks to my teammates and mentors for encouraging my personal and professional growth!
