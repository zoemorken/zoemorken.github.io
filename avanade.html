A service design approach to improving the student quarantine process at Purdue.

Spring 2021 (5 months, January–May 2021)

Project Brief

During the Spring 2021 semester, my team worked with Avanade on a project as part of our Experience Studio course at Purdue University. We were tasked to create a digital platform to serve as a source of authoritative guidance for the city of West Lafayette. To do this, we decided to focus our efforts on helping students at Purdue University (located in West Lafayette) navigate the quarantine process confidently.

My Role

I co-led a team of seven UX Design students and worked closely with our sponsors. I was responsible for conducting interviews with stakeholders, synthesizing results from team ideation sessions, and designing the mockups for our finalized concept. As a team lead, I built and executed the sprint schedule to complete milestones as well as facilitated remote team activities.

Project Goals
  1. Explore common problems Purdue students face when seeking answers for individual COVID-19 encounters.
  2. Identify students’ pain points with the current resources available to them – namely Protect Purdue.
  3. Propose new ways to bridge the gap of misunderstanding between students and sought-after virus information.
  4. Deliver a solution that allows the university to reduce confusion / frustration around the quarantine process, ultimately promoting a safer living environment.

Stakeholders

The two main stakeholders we focused on for the majority of the project were students at Purdue and nurse case managers. Though our solution is geared toward helping students navigate quarantine smoothly, it was important to take into account the needs and goals of case managers so that Protect Purdue could improve its operations as a whole. Goals and frustrations for each were primarily informed by our interview findings.

User Story

Note: The story presented below was modeled according to Purdue’s quarantine process during the Spring 2021 semester. Please refer to the summary at the end of this page for what this process entails.

Problems Identified
  • What’s being communicated to students during their first phone call with a case manager. Students like David can be overwhelmed by the amount of information thrown at them, especially when they have just learned that they need to move into quarantine housing.
  • Making the timeline and structure of quarantine known to students.Once moved in, students don’t know what they should be doing to set themselves up for success in their temporary living environment.
  • Improving flow and mode of communication between students and case managers. Students and case managers may not get the answers they need in time when using asynchronous forms of communication such as email. Calling is unideal due to the fact that students must wait to be redirected to their case manager.

How might we get students the information they need when in quarantine?

Solution

Our solution tackles two points in David’s journey: before he moves (transition period) and during his stay at Purdue Village (quarantine period). The concept proposed for the transition period deals primarily with how students receive information from Protect Purdue’s website in a time-sensitive situation. On the other hand, the concept proposed for the quarantine period is a tool that would be seamlessly integrated with a student’s Purdue Career (known as “myPurdue”) account.

Research

We began our project by attempting to identify a specific user group within the Purdue student body. Though we had decided to focus on COVID-19 confusion at Purdue University, we believed that narrowing in on a distinct population (whether that be related to living situation, class standing, etc.) would be beneficial in coming up with a solution that addressed their specific needs. Before deciding on a group, we first wanted to understand the true breadth of problems students face due to COVID. In doing so, our goal was to have a grasp on what issues exist on campus before narrowing our focus.

Surveys and Interviews

We began our research with a survey to send out to the general student population. The goal of using a survey was to identify patterns in problems that various student groups across campus face. Respondents that were interested in participating in a follow-up interview were contacted to share more about their personal experiences or anecdotes regarding COVID-related issues at Purdue.

We created our survey using Qualtrics and sent it out to various group chats, Discord servers, and the Purdue subreddit. After leaving it open for one week, our team received a total of 146 responses. Some questions that yielded interesting insights were:

  • How safe do you feel at Purdue in regards to COVID-19?
  • Do you feel like Purdue’s guidelines have been effective in protecting the school?
  • Have you experienced any confusion about the COVID-19 protocol while at Purdue?
Findings
  • Most students experience confusion on when to quarantine due to second or third hand exposure. Since there is no clear-cut definition of second or third hand exposure, students are unsure which category they fall into and what steps to take next.
  • Protect Purdue fails to adequately update students on policy changes. Protect Purdue tries to update students through emails or website updates, but this does not motivate students to read policy changes in depth.
  • Students don’t know specific regulations for different social settings (e.g. dining courts, dorm rooms, private study rooms, etc.). Students are confused on which guidelines apply in different settings and if there are exceptions.
  • There is no direct correlation between where students are living on campus, and their general confusion with the Protect Purdue guidelines. Regardless of their living situation, students were confused on the same issues.

Based on our survey findings, we were surprised to discover that there was no correlation between a student’s living arrangement and the type of confusion they experience with Protect Purdue guidelines and procedures. Our follow-up interviews demonstrated that there were many opportunities for improvement within the quarantine and isolation process, the greatest being students didn’t know when to quarantine. Because of this, we decided to investigate the process of quarantining at Purdue.

Key Terms

  • Nurse Case Manager – The first point of contact when the Protect Purdue Health Center (PPHC) determines a student has or is at risk of contracting the virus. Students are assigned a case manager who decides on an appropriate course of care and monitors the student’s health regularly when in quarantine or isolation.
  • Protect Purdue – Purdue University’s comprehensive strategy to keep the campus and community safe by limiting the spread of COVID-19. More information can be found here.
  • Purdue Career Account (myPurdue) – A portal that allows students to manage their financial aid, academics and more during their time at Purdue.
