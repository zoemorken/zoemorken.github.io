Walmart Internship

Mapped the Product Owner engagement experience with the AXID teams.

Remote, Summer 2021 (3 months, June–August 2021)

Note

My work is currently under NDA. If you’d like to know more, please reach out to me.

Although I cannot publicly disclose the projects I was working on with Walmart, I had a wonderful experience collaborating with various designers, project managers, and business leaders.

The Logistics

I interned on the Associate Experience, Innovation, and Design (AXID) team for 10 weeks (June-August 2021). The AXID team serves as an in-house UX consultancy for various Product Owners within Walmart Global Tech. Because the team was beginning the process of major restructuring, I was given the important task of taking a service design approach to investigate the team's collaboration with Product Owners. My deliverables were a comprehensive journey map in FigJam (Figma's beta whiteboarding tool) as well as a recommendations document organized by stakeholders needed to implement the suggestion and anticipated difficulty. To do this, I conducted stakeholder interviews with internal and external cross-functional partners (Senior Directors, Product Owners, and Software Developers) to determine user needs and frustrations. In addition, I built high-fidelity prototypes for an internal enterprise tool set to release Q4 of 2021. Though remote, my internship was fast-paced from working on my internship project to participating in design meetings and bi-weekly sprint planning.

Reflection

With this being my third time interning remote, it was important for me to make sure I was getting the most out of a remote internship by meeting new people. To do this, I took the initiative by scheduling calls with people on my team as well as other teams within our department. Albeit intimidating, I found that taking the time to familiarize myself with people and their role in Walmart (including Product Owners, software developers, project managers) was necessary to build lasting relationships. The internship project I was given also required me to branch out. I also found it helpful for both myself and my team to send consistent progress updates to outside of our weekly team check-ins. I also let designers on my team know when I had completed certain deliverables and needed feedback. In doing so, I tried my best not to leave anyone wondering what I was up to.
