Hello, I'm Zoë!

I am a student at Purdue University studying User Experience Design with minors in Communication and Psychology, graduating December 2021. I am currently seeking internship and full-time opportunities for 2022.

When I first stepped foot into the studio of Purdue’s undergraduate UX program, I had no idea of the challenging yet rewarding journey ahead of me. Like many of my peers, my understanding of the UX discipline was limited to designing aesthetically-pleasing interfaces for mobile and web applications. Now, three years later, UX has come to hold a special meaning for me. It is ultimately about people: their backgrounds, personalities, beliefs, values, and motivations. An experience's ability to impact is only as good as the designer's understanding of the person beforehand.

My Design Philosophy

Though designing for no two problems look alike, I approach each with the following:

Empathy

It’s an often-misused term, but true empathy involves reducing the distance between you and the person you’re designing for. As designers, we have the privilege of entering into another’s world. Coming to the realization that our users are just as complex as us is both overwhelming and liberating. Therefore, it’s critical that we remember our design will interact with people and their contexts, not just consumers of products.

Inventiveness

It takes courage to be bold. It also takes discernment knowing whether to make tweaks to an existing solution or completely challenge the status quo. Despite the research indicating which path to take, either can be transformed in ways that enhance the current experience. I am an imaginative yet detail-oriented designer who loves dreaming up new ways to solve familiar problems.

Humility

I recognize that I don’t have it all figured out. The end outcome can always be better, and areas of the design process could always use extra time to be explored further. Learning to accept the invaluable critique I’ve received from peers and mentors over the years has promoted my growth as a designer. I am always eager to learn and expand my range of skills, but I have come to embrace that this is an ongoing, lifelong pursuit.
